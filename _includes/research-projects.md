    <div class="experience-content">
      <h4>GROWD: Generative Modelling of Dense Crowds under Multiscale Observations</h4>
      <figure class="fellowship-figure">
        <img src="../assets/img/bienvenue_plus_funding.jpg" alt="Bienvenüe+ MSCA-COFUND Fellowship funding information">
      </figure>
      <p>GROWD is my Bienvenüe+ MSCA-COFUND Fellowship project, hosted by the VirtUs team at Inria Rennes. The project develops probabilistic generative models for dense crowd dynamics by combining sparse, local, fine-grained agent-level observations with dense, global, coarse-grained video-derived measurements.</p>
      <p>The project addresses a key limitation in current crowd modelling: existing approaches often either assume unrealistic access to complete individual trajectories or rely on coarse field-level observations that obscure local interactions. GROWD treats these two sources of information as complementary views of the same underlying crowd dynamics.</p>

  <div class="experience-card">
    <div class="experience-badge">Questions</div>
    <div class="experience-content">
      <h4>Research Questions</h4>
      <ul>
        <li>How can dense crowds be represented under partial observability, where only sparse individual-level motion and coarse video-level measurements are available?</li>
        <li>How can generative models capture uncertainty, stochasticity, and multiple plausible crowd evolutions?</li>
        <li>How can learned crowd models support inverse reasoning between desired crowd patterns and environmental or organisational arrangements?</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="experience-card">
    <div class="experience-badge">Objectives</div>
    <div class="experience-content">
      <h4>Project Objectives</h4>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>Multiscale Data Construction:</strong> Build a dataset that aligns sparse agent-level motion observations with dense video-derived field-level measurements.</li>
        <li><strong>Generative Modelling:</strong> Develop a probabilistic generative framework for modelling and analysing dense crowd dynamics from multiscale observations.</li>
        <li><strong>Inverse Reasoning:</strong> Explore how trained generative models can reason from desired crowd patterns back to plausible environmental or organisational conditions.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>
  </div>

  <div class="experience-card">
    <div class="experience-badge">Work Plan</div>
    <div class="experience-content">
      <h4>Work Packages</h4>
      <ul>
        <li><strong>WP1:</strong> Multiscale data construction from existing host-institution datasets and new field data collection where feasible.</li>
        <li><strong>WP2:</strong> Generative modelling and pattern analysis of dense crowds using structured representations of crowd motion.</li>
        <li><strong>WP3:</strong> Inverse reasoning for crowd-aware planning and management using the trained generative model.</li>
      </ul>
    </div>

      <h4>Expected Contributions</h4>

        <li>Generative models that represent uncertainty and behavioural diversity beyond average crowd behaviour.</li>
        <li>Analysis tools for exploring how environmental arrangements may influence crowd dynamics in high-density public spaces.</li>
